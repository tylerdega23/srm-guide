SRM Case Study: Establishing Look-Back Window & Rest Level

This case study walks through a real-world example using JCT screenshots to illustrate how to determine the 24-hour look-back window, calculate total flight time, and apply CFR 121.471 rest level requirements.

Step 1: Review Segment Duty Periods

We begin by identifying a crewmember’s two consecutive segments. The JCT tracking view shows rest between segments and key times like block, report, and release.

Step 2: Identify the Look-Back Window Timeline

Using the block-in time of the SDF–CLT segment (09:53z/8z), we count backward 24 hours to 09:53z/7z. This becomes our official look-back window.

Step 3: Evaluate Rest in the Look-Back Window

JCT shows that the crewmember was off duty beginning at 07:47z/8z, and our look-back window starts at 09:53z/7z.

Rest Calculation:

  • Look-back window: 09:53z/7z → 09:53z/8z
  • Off-duty start: 07:47z/8z
  • Rest = 09:53z/8z - 09:53z/7z = 24:00, minus duty ending at 07:47z/8z → 21:54 rest

This exceeds the minimum required Scheduled Rest. Therefore, the crewmember is not in a reduced rest period and does not require Mandatory Rest.

Step 4: Calculate Total Flight Time in Window

The only flight falling inside this 24-hour window is UPS 6286 with 1:06 block time. This determines the required rest level category.

Step 5: Apply to SRM Rest Matrix

According to the matrix, 1:06 of flight time falls into Level 1 Rest:

  • Scheduled Rest: 9 hrs
  • Reduced Rest: 8 hrs
  • Mandatory Rest: 10 hrs (if reduced rest taken)

Important: If the crewmember had received only 8 hours of rest, this would qualify as Reduced Rest. In that case, Mandatory Rest of 10 hours must begin within 24 hours of the start of the reduced rest period (i.e., no later than 07:47z/9z).

Final Review:

The crewmember’s rest of 21:54 in this scenario clearly exceeds the 9-hour scheduled minimum. No Mandatory Rest is needed, and the flight segment is CFR compliant.
